approach

Intentional methods, not opaque heuristics

How I move from research questions to reliable systems.

principles

  • Interrogate the data-generating process before model selection.
  • Prioritize explainability and observability from the first prototype.
  • Bias toward robustness: test for drift, stress extreme regimes, and document failure modes.
research & data

Interrogate signals before models. Clean, align, and pressure-test assumptions with domain context.

modeling & experimentation

Iterate with interpretable baselines first; layer in complexity only when marginal gains justify it.

evaluation & uncertainty

Calibration, sensitivity analysis, and failure-mode surfacing are first-class outputs.

communication

Translate results into decisions with visual, inspectable artifacts — not opaque heuristics.

about

Carter Daly — student & research-oriented ML practitioner

Focused on fusion, race strategy, and time-series — always with interpretable, decision-ready outputs.

I study and build ML systems where stakes are high and signals are messy — stabilizing fusion plasmas, plotting F1 race strategies, and forecasting uncertain time-series. My bias: clarity over cleverness, physics and priors over unchecked complexity.

Outside experiments, I prototype interfaces that make model behavior legible to teams. I’m currently extending this portfolio into bespoke case studies.
